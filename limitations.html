Step 7 of 8: Limitations

Why Limitations Matter

Even the most advanced fall detection systems face real-world challenges. These limitations arise from hardware constraints, environmental conditions, user behavior, and regulatory restrictions. Understanding them is crucial for designing reliable, user-friendly systems that minimize false alarms and maximize safety.

üåô Environmental Challenges

Cameras struggle in low light, with glare, or occlusion. Environmental factors significantly impact pose detection accuracy.

  • Low Light: Reduced contrast and increased noise
  • Occlusion: Furniture blocking body parts
  • Placement: Suboptimal camera angles
  • Mitigation: IR cameras, torch mode, IMU fusion
Camera-based systems degrade in challenging environments

  • Threshold Issues: Hard-coded limits fail
  • Mitigation: Personalized calibration, adaptive ML

üîí Privacy & Policy

Privacy concerns and Play Store restrictions [6] limit system capabilities and user acceptance.

  • Video Privacy: Always-on camera concerns
  • SMS Restrictions: Play Store permission limits
  • User Trust: Surveillance fears

Privacy-first design builds user trust

Fall detection is promising, but real-world deployment must address challenges in environment, algorithms, user behavior, and privacy. Acknowledging these limitations guides better research and design.
